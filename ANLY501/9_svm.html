2. Impact from Chia on Drives Stock

2.1 Feature Extraction

A monthly offer count is used for tracking the stock trend in 2021. Monthly offer counts from February to October are extracted from the Amazon database and normalized by divide by the offer count in January. The brand, drive_type, drive_subtype, and capacity are also extracted from database and used for further analysis.

Then, 60 drives are selected as training set and manually labeled whether their stocks are affected by Chia (by looking at the entire daily offer count history using Keepa API). As a result, 25 drives are labeled as 1 (IMPACT) and 35 drives are labeled as 0 (NO IMPACT).

2.2 Model Training & Effect Analysis

First, three SVM with different kernels are trained by the training set. Costs of the models are selected based on validation set accuracy. The final models are linear kernel with cost=0.01, radial kernel with cost=2, and sigmoid kernel with cost=0.05. The confusion matrices are shown below.

The final model used to label the test set is the linear kernel model with cost=0.01, which shows a great classification power of CHIA IMPACT. As a result, 81 drives in test set are labeled as 1 (IMPACT) and 147 drives are labeled as 0 (NO IMPACT).

Next, the offer count time series of drives are visualized according to the label CHIA_IMPACT.

Also, the distribution of stocks at January 2021 is visualized based on CHIA_IMPACT.

2.3 Summary of Effect Analysis on Drive & Chia using SVM

In this section, svm model is used to predict whether the stock(offer count) of drive is impacted by Chia coin. From the visualization and analysis above, the conclusion is listed below:

1. Around 40% drives are impacted on stock by Chia.

2. Their marketplace offer counts slumped during April~August in 2021.

3. Average stocks of the impacted drives is significantly larger than not impacted ones.
